🎯 Model Selection Criteria

Binary stage (Stage 1): Only models with ROC-AUC > 0.85 are used for malware detection (8 of 20 trained models). Models trained on extremely imbalanced datasets (e.g., 99.99% malicious) produce near-random ROC-AUC (~0.50) and would corrupt the ensemble voting with false positives.

Multiclass stage (Stage 2): All 20 trained models are included for variant identification. Some attack variants (e.g., Torii C&C, Mirai C&C, C&C-HeartBeat-Attack) only appear in specific datasets, so all models are needed for complete variant coverage. The confidence-weighted voting system automatically prioritizes predictions from the most accurate models, ensuring reliable results even with lower-accuracy models included.
